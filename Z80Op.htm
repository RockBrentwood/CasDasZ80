Cycles Flags (SZYHXPNC)
Cycles: additions apply if the conditional is true.

Flags affected are always shown in S Z Y H X P/V N C order, with the following markings:
  • "0" means the flag is reset after the instruction.
  • "1" means the flag is set after the instruction.
  • "-" means the flag is not changed.
  • "S", "Z", "Y", "H", "X", "N" or "C" mean the flag is directly affected as part of the function, itself.
  • P/V behaves like even parity flag when marked "P" and like overflow flag when marked "V".
Undocumented instructions are marked by * in front of the their name.
If they are useful they are put into particular instruction group marked by cell background color.
If they are mere aliases for other instruction they are marked by grey background.
In combinations of prefixes the only useful ones are 335 313 and 375 313.
335 355 and 375 355 behave like 355 alone, so no instructions like adc IX,BC exist.
In a consecutive row of 335 and 375 prefixes the last one prevails.
